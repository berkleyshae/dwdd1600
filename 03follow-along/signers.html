Who were the Signers

Most Famous Signers

Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence.
Later became the 3rd President of the United States (1801–1809).
Legacy includes the Louisiana Purchase and founding the University of Virginia.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
Strong advocate for independence in the Continental Congress.
Later became the 2nd President of the United States (1797–1801).
Helped draft the Treaty of Paris and served as a key diplomat in Europe.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
Renowned scientist, inventor, and diplomat.
Helped secure French support for the American Revolution.
One of the most influential Founding Fathers; featured on the $100 bill.
John Hancock (Massachusetts)
President of the Continental Congress.
Known for his bold and prominent signature on the Declaration.
His name became synonymous with the word “signature.”
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
Early leader in the movement for independence.
Helped organize resistance through the Sons of Liberty and the Boston Tea Party.
Strong advocate for liberty and revolutionary action.
Roger Sherman (Connecticut)
Only person to sign all four major U.S. founding documents: the Continental Association, Declaration of Independence, Articles of Confederation, and Constitution.
Played a major role in shaping the structure of the U.S. government.
Robert Morris (Pennsylvania)
Key financier of the American Revolution.
Used personal credit and resources to support the Continental Army.
Later served as Superintendent of Finance.
Charles Carroll (Maryland)
The only Catholic signer of the Declaration of Independence.
Last surviving signer; died in 1832.
Advocate for religious freedom and early supporter of American independence.
James Wilson (Pennsylvania)
Signer of both the Declaration of Independence and the U.S. Constitution.
Later served as one of the first Justices on the U.S. Supreme Court.
Influential in shaping legal and constitutional interpretation.
